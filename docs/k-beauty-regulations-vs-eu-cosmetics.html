Korean vs EU Cosmetic Regulations Compared

MFDS vs EC 1223/2009 · Side-by-side comparison · Updated April 2026

Korea and the EU both have comprehensive cosmetic ingredient regulation systems. They share the same goal — consumer safety — but they've made very different decisions on specific ingredients. If you're a brand selling in both markets, understanding these differences is essential.

Where Korea Is Stricter

Salicylic Acid — Age restriction: Korea prohibits use in products for children under 13 (except shampoo). The EU sets the limit at under 3. This is the strictest age restriction for Salicylic Acid in the world.

Functional cosmetic classification: Korea requires certain active ingredients (retinol, arbutin, niacinamide at active levels) to be registered as "functional cosmetics" (기능성화장품) with additional safety documentation. The EU doesn't have an equivalent category — these are regulated as normal cosmetics with concentration limits.

Where the EU Is Stricter

Isobutylparaben — Banned vs. allowed: The EU banned Isobutylparaben entirely (Annex II) due to insufficient safety data. Korea still allows it at 0.4%. A product containing Isobutylparaben that's legal in Korea would be illegal in the EU.

Propylparaben — Lower limit: The EU reduced the Propylparaben limit from 0.4% to 0.14% in 2014. Korea maintains 0.4%. Same data, different regulatory decision.

Fragrance allergen labeling: The EU requires 80+ fragrance allergens (expanded from 26 in 2023, including Linalool, Limonene, Citronellol) to be individually declared on product labels above certain thresholds. Korea has no equivalent labeling requirement for fragrance allergens.

Total banned substances: The EU's Annex II contains 1,700+ prohibited substances compared to Korea's 172+. Many of these are industrial chemicals unlikely to appear in cosmetics, but the regulatory scope is significantly broader.

What This Means for K-Beauty Brands Exporting to the EU

If you're a Korean cosmetic brand entering the EU market, these are the key compliance checkpoints:

1. Check every ingredient against EU Annex II. The EU bans 10x more substances than Korea. An ingredient that's "Not Listed" in Korea could be prohibited in the EU.

2. Review preservative concentrations. Propylparaben and Butylparaben limits are lower in the EU (0.14% vs Korea's 0.4%). Isobutylparaben must be removed entirely.

3. Add fragrance allergen labeling. If your product contains any fragrance, you likely need to declare individual allergens like Linalool, Limonene, and Citronellol on the EU label.

4. Check age-specific restrictions. Korea's under-13 Salicylic Acid rule is stricter than the EU's, so products already compliant in Korea should be fine — but verify other ingredients where the EU is stricter.
