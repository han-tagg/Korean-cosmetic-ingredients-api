Salicylic Acid Cosmetic Regulation by Country

INCI: Salicylic Acid · CAS: 69-72-7 · Data from 10 countries · Updated April 2026

Salicylic Acid is one of the most widely used cosmetic ingredients — found in acne treatments, exfoliants, shampoos, and anti-dandruff products. It's regulated as a preservative in some countries and as an active ingredient in others. The concentration limits vary dramatically.

Japan stands out with a 0.2% limit across all cosmetics — ten times lower than what Korea and Canada allow. A product formulated at 2% Salicylic Acid for the Korean market would need to be completely reformulated for Japan.

Korea has the strictest age restriction in the world for Salicylic Acid — under 13, compared to under 3 in most other countries. Japan, despite having the lowest concentration limit, has no age restriction at all.

Why This Matters

If you're formulating a Salicylic Acid product for international markets, you can't use one formula everywhere. A face wash at 2% Salicylic Acid marketed to teenagers would be compliant in the EU, China, and Canada — but prohibited in Korea for anyone under 13, and over the limit in Japan.
